THE HCCS SEAL (School's Logo)


The H.C.C.S. seal or logo carries the emblem of the Santo Niño inside the circle, with the inscription “Sapientia Aetate Et Gratia” and “JHS 1945” embedded at the bottom of the figure of Holy Child Jesus.

The above caption simply means that Holy Child Catholic School is a place where every child grows in WISDOM (not only in academic but also in knowledge of God), in AGE (growing not only in age but in health and body, and in spirit), and in HOLINESS (to be pleasing to God – (maging kalugud-lugod sa mata ng Diyos at sa paningin ng kapwa)

  The color blue represents loyalty to the teachings of the Church and to the ideals of its founder. The color white represents purity of mind and heart.


PHILOSOPHY OF EDUCATION


Catholic education is a concrete expression of the mission entrusted by Christ to the Church He founded.

Through education, the Church seeks to inform her members by proclaiming the Good News, to form their hearts by living the Good News and to transform their lives by translating this proclamation into action.

With Religion as the core of the curriculum, HOLY CHILD CATHOLIC SCHOOL is obliged to contribute in its own way towards the realization of this mission, through the programs adapted to the needs of its stakeholders.



As a parochial school, HOLY CHILD CATHOLIC SCHOOL, is inevitably an integral part of the pastoral plan of the Local Church by providing for the formal Christian education and formation of the faithful to become conscienticized citizens in the service of God and country.

As a private basic-education institution, HOLY CHILD CATHOLIC SCHOOL offers excellent education to equip the students with the knowledge, skills and values necessary to make them responsible, productive and globally competitive citizens in a technologically-driven society.
